## #98 + #99 — Heatmap sum aggregation & configurable week start (`feat(plugin)`)

- `aggregateForHeatmap` takes an `aggregationMethod` (average default, sum
  optional), reusing the chart path's `combineValues`. Heatmap added to
  `AGGREGATION_METHOD_SUPPORTED_TYPES`, so the average/sum dropdown appears for
  heatmaps in the card menu and presets; threaded through `HeatmapConfig` and
  the config helper.
- New `weekStartsOn` plugin setting (Monday default, Sunday optional) with a
  "First day of the week" dropdown. `date.utils` exposes
  `setWeekStartDay`/`getWeekStartDay`; `startOfWeek`, `isSameWeek`,
  `getWeeksBetween` and the this/last-week time frames honor it. Heatmap day
  labels follow the start (Mon/Wed/Fri vs Sun/Tue/Thu). ISO week parsing/labels
  stay Monday-based (ISO-8601). Plugin applies it on load and on settings change.
- (`weekStartsOn` was the request "Monday as the default" attached to #98; it is
  filed separately as #99.)

## Re-render pipeline fixes (no issue — live testing)

- **Config changes now apply without a full-grid rebuild** (`fix(plugin)`):
  color/scale/aggregation/reference-line/moving-average/heatmap-option changes
  previously did nothing until the next data update (the incremental fast path
  ignores config-only changes). New `applyConfigChange()` rebuilds just the
  affected card in place (recreating the visualization with fresh config);
  falls back to a forced full render (`forceFullRender` flag consumed by the
  render path) when the card can't be isolated (multi-viz, overlay-hidden, or a
  preset-derived ephemeral id). Preset edits also force the full path.
- **Debounced rebuilds** (`perf(plugin)`): `onDataUpdated` coalesces bursts
  (vault load, bulk edits) into one rebuild after 150ms. The synchronous
  grid-settings / drag-drop guard still runs before scheduling; timer cleared on
  unload. Body moved to `rebuildView()`.
- **Visual stability during full re-renders** (`fix(plugin)`): the full path now
  reserves the previous content height and overlays a spinner
  (`.lt-rebuild-overlay`) until the async card render completes, then releases
  both — no more collapse/flicker. Cleared on unload.
