## Re-render pipeline fixes (no issue — live testing)

- **Config changes now apply without a full-grid rebuild** (`fix(plugin)`):
  color/scale/aggregation/reference-line/moving-average/heatmap-option changes
  previously did nothing until the next data update (the incremental fast path
  ignores config-only changes). New `applyConfigChange()` rebuilds just the
  affected card in place (recreating the visualization with fresh config);
  falls back to a forced full render (`forceFullRender` flag consumed by the
  render path) when the card can't be isolated (multi-viz, overlay-hidden, or a
  preset-derived ephemeral id). Preset edits also force the full path.
- **Debounced rebuilds** (`perf(plugin)`): `onDataUpdated` coalesces bursts
  (vault load, bulk edits) into one rebuild after 150ms. The synchronous
  grid-settings / drag-drop guard still runs before scheduling; timer cleared on
  unload. Body moved to `rebuildView()`.
- **Visual stability during full re-renders** (`fix(plugin)`): the full path now
  reserves the previous content height and overlays a spinner
  (`.lt-rebuild-overlay`) until the async card render completes, then releases
  both — no more collapse/flicker. Cleared on unload.

## Dependency vulnerability advisories (`fix(deps)`)

Cleared all advisories reported by `bun audit` (34 → 0), all in dev/build/lint
tooling (none ship in the bundled `main.js`).

- The patched versions for most flagged packages are **within the existing
  semver ranges** their consumers declare, so a lockfile re-resolve to
  latest-in-range fixed them without forcing cross-major changes:
  ajv 6.12.6→6.15.0 (8.x kept), minimatch 3.1.2→3.1.5 (8.x/9.x kept),
  brace-expansion 1.1.12→1.1.15 (2.x→2.1.1), picomatch 2.3.1→2.3.2 (4.x→4.0.4),
  handlebars 4.7.8→4.7.9, lodash 4.17.21→4.18.1, js-yaml 4.1.1→4.2.0,
  yaml 2.8.2→2.9.0, flatted 3.3.3→3.4.2, fast-uri 3.1.0→3.1.2.
  `@conventional-changelog/git-client` 1.0.1→2.7.0 came along via
  git-raw-commits 5.0.0→5.0.1 / git-semver-tags 8.0.0→8.0.1 (within range).
- **`tmp`** is the only exception: `external-editor` pins `^0.0.33` and the fix
  (0.2.4+) is out of that range, so added a single `overrides` entry in
  `package.json` (`"tmp": "^0.2.4"` → resolves 0.2.7). This is the **only**
  package.json change.
- **bun limitation found:** bun's `overrides`/`resolutions` are flat-only — no
  version-selector (`name@range`) or nested/parent-scoped keys; a flat override
  clobbers every major. That's why the multi-major packages were fixed via
  re-resolution, not overrides.
- **Gotcha:** `rm bun.lock && bun install` re-resolves the `latest`-tagged deps
  (`obsidian`, `@types/bun`), bumping obsidian 1.12.3→1.13.1 whose types demand
  new `override` modifiers (tsc errors). Pinned both during the resolve, then
  restored `"latest"` in package.json and the lockfile root spec (resolved
  versions stay 1.12.3/1.3.4) — matching the committed pattern; `bun install` is
  a no-op. Keep obsidian at 1.12.3 here.

Verified: `bun audit` clean, tsc/lint/test (557)/build green, commitlint
validate+reject, commitizen/external-editor load, `release:changelog` generates.
